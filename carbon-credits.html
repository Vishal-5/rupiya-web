Carbon Credits

Turn better soil into verified revenue

What is a Carbon Credit?

One Credit = One Tonne CO₂

A carbon credit represents one tonne of carbon dioxide equivalent (CO₂e) removed from the atmosphere or prevented from being released.

Trade & Monetize

Companies and organizations buy carbon credits to offset their emissions, creating a marketplace for climate-positive practices.

Verified & Certified

All credits are measured, verified, and certified by third-party auditors following international standards.

How Regenerative Agriculture Creates Credits

Four key practices that sequester carbon in your soil

Cover Crops

Planting cover crops between main seasons keeps carbon in the soil and prevents erosion

Minimal Tillage

Reducing soil disturbance preserves soil structure and keeps carbon locked underground

Crop Rotation

Diverse crop rotations improve soil health and increase organic carbon storage

Composting

Adding organic matter back to soil builds carbon-rich humus and improves fertility
